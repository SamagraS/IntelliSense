1.Executive Summary
Ugro Capital Limited has been assessed for a Working Capital facility of ₹25 Crores. The analysis recommends Approval With Conditions for a reduced limit of ₹18 Crores at Base Rate + 180bps. The primary risk factors are elevated promoter pledge at 68.4% — above the high-risk threshold of 60% — and an active DRT case linked via director relationship graph. DSCR of 1.08x is below the policy minimum of 1.25x, limiting the sanctionable amount to ₹18 Crores at which DSCR clears the threshold. These risks are partially mitigated by strong collateral coverage of 1.8x with primary immovable property that is unencumbered per MCA records.
2.Company and Promoter Profile
Ugro Capital Limited (CIN: L67190MH2007PLC169058) is a listed NBFC incorporated in 2007, focused on data-driven SME secured lending with an AUM of approximately ₹6,800 Crores as of Q3 FY2025. The company is promoted by Shachindra Nath (MD & CEO) and has a board comprising 6 directors, of whom 2 are identified as independent directors under applicable governance norms.
The promoter group holds 28.3% of total equity, of which 68.4% is pledged as collateral for personal borrowings — a material governance concern that has been weighted above baseline in this assessment per override by the credit manager. Director Rajesh Kumar is also a director of XYZ Infrastructure Limited, which has two active Debt Recovery Tribunal cases totalling ₹8.4 Crores before Bombay High Court, filed December 2024.
3.Industry and Conditions
The NBFC sector faces tightening regulatory conditions following the RBI's December 2024 circular mandating higher provisioning norms for SME NPA accounts, which is expected to compress near-term profitability across the sector. Sector-wide NPA levels in the SME segment showed an uptick in Q3 FY2025, with the industry median gross NPA rising to approximately 4.2% from 3.6% in the prior quarter. Ugro Capital's differentiated, data-driven underwriting model is cited as a competitive advantage, with the company maintaining approximately 2.1% market share in secured SME lending.

Character (5.2 / 10):

Revenue growth is strong at 24.3% CAGR. However, DSCR at 1.08x is below the policy minimum of 1.25x, GST-bank divergence of 18.3% in 4 months raises revenue reliability concerns, and site visit notes confirm operating below benchmark capacity utilisation at 40–65%.

Collateral (7.8 / 10):
Collateral coverage of 1.8x with immovable property is strong. MCA charges registry confirms no conflicting encumbrances on the proposed security. Site visit confirmed property in good condition, owner-occupied.
Conditions (6.0 / 10):
Regulatory headwinds from the RBI's December 2024 provisioning circular and sector-wide NPA stress are partially offset by the company's differentiated market position and demonstrated underwriting discipline relative to peers.
